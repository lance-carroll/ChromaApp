# CHROMA WORD ENGINE — Playtest Rules (v2)

---

## The Core Idea

Chroma is a play-by-post RPG where **the post is the turn**. You write a complete post, incorporate mechanical Words into it, resolve the action, and leave the fiction changed — all in one go. No waiting for the GM between declaring and resolving.

Play proceeds in **Beats** — loose async rounds. The GM frames the situation, every active player posts once (in any order), the GM updates, and the next Beat begins. Your group sets a posting window (12–72 hours). If you can't post, use a **Ghost Post** so the game doesn't stall.

---

## Words and Chroma

A **Word** is a mechanically significant word you incorporate into your post. Words come from your Core Words, your hand of Cards, the current Scene Words, your Gear, Wounds, Setup Words, or Stitch.

Some Words have a **Chroma** — a symbolic color. Card Words, Scene Words, Wound Words, and Chroma Gear always have Chroma. Core Words and mundane Gear Words are usually **colorless** unless upgraded, stained, or otherwise specified. Colorless Words cannot satisfy Chroma requirements.

| Token | Chroma | Themes |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 🟥 | Red | Violence, hunger, urgency, desire, pursuit |
| 🟦 | Blue | Memory, reflection, logic, secrets, distance |
| 🟩 | Green | Growth, body, healing, dependency, rot |
| 🟨 | Gold | Authority, commerce, status, attention, pride |
| 🟪 | Violet | Dream, magic, prophecy, distortion, thresholds |
| ⬛ | Black | Death, fear, loss, guilt, secrecy, endings |
| ⬜ | White | Mercy, oath, truth, judgment, devotion |

Words also have **Tags** — descriptors that determine what Recipes they can activate (e.g., *violence, oath, passage, deception*). Tags are metadata; you don't write them into your post.

### Chroma Sources

When a Recipe requires Chroma, you supply it from one of three source types:

- **Spend** — Card Words from your hand. Discarded after use. Your primary fuel.
- **Tap** — Scene Words (once per post, not consumed) and Chroma Gear (once per scene). Available but limited.
- **Risk** — Wound Words. Satisfies one Chroma requirement, but the GM gains 1 Threat in the Wound's matching bucket (see Threat Buckets). On a Miss, the Wound worsens or creates a new complication.

Setup Words from other players follow the rules of their source: colorless by default, Chroma if the creator infused them (see Setup posts).

## Your Character

### Core Words

You have **4 Core Words** — your reliable verbs, instincts, or methods. Each has 2–3 tags. Core Words are always available and never consumed. They're your fallback when your hand, the scene, and your gear don't supply the tags you need.

### Resources

| Resource | Start | Max | Notes |
|----------|-------|-----|-------|
| **Focus** | 3 | 3 | Spent to Act. Recovered via Breathe posts and doubles. |
| **Thread** | 0 | 3 | Earned through Stitch and Breakthroughs. Spent to intensify Recipes, soften consequences, draw Cards, or weaponize Wounds. |
| **Hand** | 4 | 4 | Your available Card Words. Draw to hand size at scene start. |
| **Wounds** | 0 | 3 | Lasting harm expressed as Words. When all slots are full and you'd take another, you're **Taken Out**. |

### Deck

21 Cards chosen during character creation. You are not required to take equal numbers from each Chroma — weight your deck toward the colors your Recipes demand. Your deck composition shapes your play style, your hand reliability, and which Threat buckets you'll feed the GM when you Breathe. When your deck empties, reshuffle your discard pile.

### Marks

Temporary harm — winded, shaken, watched, off-balance. Marks are your scene stamina. Most failed rolls produce Marks, not Wounds.

**Three Marks become a Wound.** When you accumulate your third Mark, clear all Marks and take a Wound instead. The GM names the Wound Word.

Marks clear at Normal scene transitions or with treatment. They carry forward through Hard transitions.

### Gear

5 Gear Slots. Gear gives permission, tags, and Gear Words — never flat bonuses.

- **Mundane Gear** is colorless. It provides tags and fictional permission.
- **Chroma Gear** has a color and may be **tapped** to satisfy one matching Chroma requirement **once per scene**.
- **Armor/Wards** have **Guard boxes**. Spend a Guard box to prevent a Mark, or reduce a Wound to a Mark. When all boxes are spent, the armor is done until repaired.
- **Supplies** use abstract Supply points. Spend 1 to produce a plausible item, create a Setup Word, clear a Mark, or support a Recipe.

Gear tags provide fictional permission and can satisfy a Recipe's Core Requirement, just like tags from any other word source.

### Tie

Your **Tie** is a single statement that pulls you in two directions — toward others and against yourself. It is both your Bond and your Burden depending on how you invoke it.

> *Example: "I will not abandon Mara" — a Bond when you risk yourself to protect her, a Burden when loyalty drags you into a fight you can't win.*

Your Tie may serve as a narrative intensifier once per scene if invoked meaningfully in your post and confirmed by the GM. (It is not one of the two mechanical intensifiers for Heightened grade — see Recipes & Rolls.)

## Post Types

Every post you write is one of these four types.

### Act

You try to change the situation under pressure.

1. **Spend 1 Focus.**
2. **Choose a Recipe.** At least one word you're incorporating must have a tag matching the Recipe's Core Requirement. This can come from Core Words, Cards, Scene Words, Gear, or any other word source.
3. **Provide Chroma components** by spending Cards, tapping Scene Words or Chroma Gear, or risking Wound Words.
4. **Determine your Grade** (see Recipes & Rolls below).
5. **Roll 2d6 + Grade bonus** vs the Challenge Rating.
6. **Resolve** inside your post. Designate a **closing word** for the next player's Stitch.

Cards spent during Act Posts **do not generate Threat**. Breathe is the normal way Card Words feed Threat.

### Setup

You create an opportunity for a later player — a held chain, a marked path, a distraction, a braced door. Some players prefer a support role; Setup is how you make your presence felt without pushing for yourself.

- Creates a **Setup Word** that persists **until used** (no expiration).
- Setup Words are **colorless by default**. A Setup Word gains Chroma if you spend a Card Word, tap a Scene Word or Chroma Gear, use a Recipe, or receive GM permission.
- May use a Recipe if the setup is risky.
- **Assist reward:** When another player uses your Setup Word to fuel an Act post, you gain **1 Thread** or **recover 1 Focus** (your choice).

In Chroma, help must exist *before* the action it helps.

### Breathe

You respond to the scene without escalating. No roll. Write a post that meaningfully engages with the scene or another player, then choose one:

- **Recover 1 Focus** — the GM gains **1 Threat** in the bucket matching the Chroma you name (must be a color in your deck).
- **Draw 1 Card** — no Threat generated.

Breathe is not a pass. It's how you stay present without pushing.

### Ghost

You can't fully participate this Beat.

> *Ghost me this Beat. Serit follows Mara and keeps watch at the rear.*

Your character stays in the fiction. You don't spend resources or suffer individual harm. **Ghost rather than disappear.**

## Stitch

If the **first meaningful word** of your post matches the **final meaningful word** of a previous post (any player or the GM), you **Stitch**.

Filler words can't Stitch (the, and, but, a, it, pronouns, etc.). Good Stitch words: *ash, bell, mercy, chain, hunger, gate, blood, silence, mirror, crown.*

The Stitch Word is **not consumed**. Multiple players can Stitch from the same word in a Beat. You can Stitch from the GM's post or from another player's already-posted closing word.

**When you Stitch, gain 1 Thread.**

---

## Scene Words

The GM provides Scene Words for each room, NPC, or situation. A Scene Word may be **tapped** to satisfy **one Chroma requirement** per post if you incorporate it meaningfully. Scene Words are not consumed — everyone can use them. **Limit: one Scene Word tapped per post.**

## Recipes and Rolls

A **Recipe** is a named action (strike, spell, social move, exploration technique). It specifies a Core Requirement (tags), a Chroma pattern, and outcomes for Hit, Strong Hit, and Miss.

### Roll

> **2d6 + Recipe Grade** vs **Challenge Rating**

### Challenge Ratings

| CR | Difficulty |
|----|-----------|
| 7 | Easy under pressure |
| 9 | Normal pressured action |
| 11 | Hard or meaningful opposition |
| 13 | Severe danger, boss obstacle |
| 15 | Desperate or mythic (rare) |

Most actions are **CR 9** or **CR 11**.

### Recipe Grades

| Grade | Requirement | Bonus |
|-------|-------------|------:|
| **Partial** | At least one matching tag + some but not all Chroma met | +0 |
| **Complete** | At least one matching tag + all required Chroma components | +2 |
| **Heightened** | Complete + one valid intensifier | +3 |

**Maximum bonus is +3.**

### Intensifiers (exactly one; only applies to a Complete Recipe)

A Complete Recipe becomes Heightened with one of these two:

- **Form** — you used a declared Form in your post (see Forms below).
- **Spend Thread** — you spend 1 Thread.

No stacking. One intensifier, one grade bump.

### Doubles

If you roll doubles (both dice show the same number), **recover 1 Focus** regardless of the roll's outcome. The rhythm of the dice gives you a second wind.

### Outcomes

| Result | Condition | Effect |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Miss** | Below CR | The action falters. You suffer a **Mark** and the GM gains **Threat** (assigned to the bucket matching the scene or Recipe). The GM may add further consequences: lost Card, Gear damage, complication, or Pressure. The scene must still change. |
| **Hit** | Meets or beats CR | You get the Recipe's basic effect. The GM adds **1 Threat** to a bucket based on the action — the effort still ripples outward. |
| **Strong Hit** | Beats CR by 3+ | You get the Recipe's Strong Hit effect. No cost. |
| **Breakthrough** | Beats CR by 6+ | You get the Strong Hit effect. Gain **1 Thread**. The GM may turn one word from your post into a **Scene Word** for the next Beat, if it makes fictional sense — your action has reshaped the environment. |

## Wounds as Words

Wounds aren't just penalties. They're dangerous language you carry.

Each Wound has a **Wound Word** with a Chroma, named by the GM when you suffer it.

**Carry the Wound:** When the GM compels your Wound Word into a post — or you choose to incorporate it showing how it limits you — choose one: recover 1 Focus, gain 1 Thread, or reduce any Threat bucket by 1.

**Weaponize the Wound:** Spend 1 Thread to use your Wound Word as a Chroma source (Risk) for a Recipe. It satisfies one matching Chroma requirement, but the GM gains 1 Threat in the Wound's matching bucket. On a Miss, the Wound worsens or creates a new complication.

---

## Forms (Optional Mastery Layer)

Forms are sentence techniques that can serve as intensifiers. Declare the Form in your post before rolling. The GM trusts declared Forms unless the claim is clearly absent or abusive.

| Form | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Invocation** | Repeated phrase at clause beginnings | *By salt I stand. By steel I answer. By blood I bar the door.* |
| **Undercut** | Understatement, negation, oblique phrasing | *The assassin's entrance was not entirely unnoticed.* |
| **Stampede** | Rush of verbs, minimal connective tissue | *I run, leap, strike, fall, rise laughing.* |
| **Paradox** | Balanced opposition | *I answer silence with thunder.* |
| **Renaming** | Compact metaphor, kenning | *I lift the wound-door and step through.* |

## Sample Recipes

### Bell-Cracker Cut — Strike
**Core Tags:** violence, weapon, force, command, or sound
**Chroma:** 🟥 + 🟨
**Hit:** Break, silence, interrupt, or disable an object, alarm, voice, weapon, or power source.
**Strong Hit:** Also remove, change, or corrupt one Scene Word.
**Miss:** It breaks loudly or dangerously. Add Threat, spend Gear, or trigger Pressure.

### Blood for the Door — Strike / Sway
**Core Tags:** violence, sacrifice, force, oath, or passage
**Chroma:** 🟥 + ⬛
**Hit:** Force passage through something that wants payment.
**Strong Hit:** You choose what it takes.
**Miss:** It opens and takes more than you offered.

### Saint-Stone Intercession — Shelter
**Core Tags:** defense, protection, oath, healing, or faith
**Chroma:** ⬜ + 🟦
**Hit:** Protect someone from supernatural, moral, psychic, or memory-based harm.
**Strong Hit:** Also reveal what the danger wanted from them.
**Miss:** You protect them, but the danger marks you instead.

### Brace the Threshold — Shelter
**Core Tags:** defense, place, passage, craft, or protection
**Chroma:** ⬜ + 🟨
**Hit:** Secure a doorway, room, camp, or vulnerable position.
**Strong Hit:** Create a Setup Word tied to the secured place.
**Miss:** It holds only if someone stays, pays, or is exposed.

### Pilgrim's Read — Uncover
**Core Tags:** memory, knowledge, perception, ritual, oath, or trace
**Chroma:** 🟦 + ⬜
**Hit:** Interpret sacred marks, vows, paths, warnings, relics, or ritual architecture.
**Strong Hit:** Ask an additional question or reveal a hidden route.
**Miss:** You learn the truth by triggering part of it.

### Follow the Slip — Uncover
**Core Tags:** memory, perception, truth, speech, suspicion, or attention
**Chroma:** 🟦 + 🟨/⬜/⬛/🟥
**Requires:** A Cue, tell, contradiction, or NPC slip.
**Hit:** Turn an NPC's slip into a concrete lead.
**Strong Hit:** They don't realize what they revealed.
**Miss:** You get the lead, but they know you noticed.

### False Key — Distort
**Core Tags:** deception, lock, secrecy, shadow, magic, or passage
**Chroma:** 🟦 + ⬛
**Hit:** Bypass, deceive, or confuse a lock, seal, ward, watcher, or gate.
**Strong Hit:** It remains fooled after you pass.
**Miss:** It opens, but something knows.

### Ashen Veil — Distort
**Core Tags:** concealment, shadow, magic, deception, fear, or death
**Chroma:** ⬛ + 🟪
**Hit:** Hide yourself, obscure an ally, or blur a scene boundary.
**Strong Hit:** Create a temporary ⬛ or 🟪 Setup Word.
**Miss:** You vanish from the wrong thing or attract something worse.

### Quiet Crossing — Move
**Core Tags:** motion, stealth, passage, patience, water, or shadow
**Chroma:** 🟦 + 🟩
**Hit:** Move through danger without raising Alert.
**Strong Hit:** Bring someone or something with you safely.
**Miss:** You arrive, but Pressure advances.

### Mercy Purchase — Sway / Shelter
**Core Tags:** mercy, trust, care, healing, debt, oath, or speech
**Chroma:** ⬜ + 🟩
**Hit:** Gain trust by addressing a need, pain, fear, or vulnerability.
**Strong Hit:** The NPC offers more than you asked for.
**Miss:** The need becomes your responsibility.

### Soft Confession — Sway
**Core Tags:** speech, truth, vulnerability, guilt, oath, or memory
**Chroma:** ⬜ + 🟦
**Hit:** Lower suspicion or make someone answer one question without hostility.
**Strong Hit:** They reveal what they wish you hadn't asked.
**Miss:** You reveal more than intended.

### Knife Under the Table — Sway / Distort
**Core Tags:** threat, fear, deception, violence, speech, or status
**Chroma:** 🟥 + ⬛
**Hit:** Force a concession through menace, blackmail, or implied violence.
**Strong Hit:** They comply without public escalation.
**Miss:** They escalate, call help, or mark you as dangerous.

## Character Creation

**1. Name and Concept** — One sentence. *Serit Vane, oathbreaker knight of the drowned gate.*

**2. Four Core Words** — Each with 2–3 tags. These are what your character *does*.

**3. Three Recipes** — At least one physical, at least one social or investigative.

**4. One or Two Forms** — Optional sentence techniques that can intensify Recipes.

**5. Tie** — One statement that serves as both your Bond (what pulls you toward others) and your Burden (what weighs on you).

**6. Starting Gear** — 1 main weapon/tool, 1 defensive item, 1 Supply Pack (Supply 3), 1 personal object, 1 open slot. One character per party may start with a Chroma Relic.

**7. Deck** — 21 Cards, chosen freely from the Card List. Weight toward your Recipes' colors or spread wide for flexibility. Draw 4 at scene start.

**8. Resources** — Focus 3, Thread 0/3, Marks 0/3, Wounds 0/3, Hand size 4, Gear Slots 5.

## GM Quick Reference

### Scene Setup

Post a **Room Card** or **NPC Card** with: description, visible features, risks, Scene Words, and an obvious Stitch Word.

### NPC Cues

Pre-authorize NPC lines and reactions so players can write complete social posts. Cue types: **Bark** (spoken line), **Tell** (visible behavior), **Offer**, **Boundary** (refusal), **Leak** (slip), **Need** (vulnerability). Cues provide fictional permission — they do not provide Chroma.

> **Example:** Cue (Leak): *"Half the town guard's been buying 'em up."* — The player uses the Cue as fictional permission for Follow the Slip, then supplies Chroma from 🟦 ledger (Card, spent) and 🟨 guard (Scene Word, tapped).

---

### Opposition

Enemies, hazards, and obstacles don't take turns. They act through the fiction the GM writes and the Threat the GM spends. There are three tiers of opposition, each with a different resolution.

#### Obstacles

One Hit resolves them. A locked door, a single guard, a collapsing bridge, a ward, a fleeing suspect. The player Acts, succeeds, and it's done. Most encounters should be Obstacles. PbP rewards decisive fiction — don't make players spend multiple real-world days on something that could resolve in one post.

#### Threats

A short progress track of **2–3 segments**. A dangerous enemy, a complex trap, a tense negotiation with real stakes.

- Each **Hit** fills 1 segment.
- Each **Strong Hit** fills 2 segments.
- A **Breakthrough** resolves the Threat outright.
- A **Miss** does not fill a segment and may add a segment at the GM's discretion (the situation worsens).

Multiple players can contribute segments in the same Beat. When all segments are filled, the Threat is resolved.

Describe the Threat shifting as segments fill — players should feel the fiction changing, not a counter ticking down. After 1 segment on a three-segment wolf pack: *"The lead wolf staggers, and the others hesitate."* After 2: *"The pack splits — two bolting, one circling."*

#### Trials

Condition-based. A boss, a siege, a ritual, a mystery, a catastrophe. The GM defines **2–4 conditions** that must be fulfilled, potentially requiring different approaches and different Recipe categories.

> **Example — The Drowned Knight:**
> - Sever its anchor chain *(Strike)*
> - Break the oath binding it *(Sway or Shelter)*
> - Name what it lost *(Uncover)* — hidden, revealed when Discovery fills

The first 1–2 conditions should be **evident from the fiction** — the GM's scene description and Scene Words make them obvious. *"The Knight rises, chain taut between its wrist and the altar stone, speaking an oath that won't end"* signals "sever the chain" and "deal with the oath" without handing over a checklist.

Remaining conditions can be **hidden behind Discovery**. Players don't know they need to name what the Knight lost until someone investigates and the Discovery track reveals it.

Conditions can be resolved in any order across multiple Beats. Each condition resolves like an Obstacle (one Hit) or a Threat (2–3 segments) depending on its complexity. Different players naturally gravitate toward different conditions based on their Recipes and deck — the combat player sees the chain, the social player hears the oath, the investigator senses something missing.

**Trial pacing target:** 2–4 Beats. Long enough to be dramatic, short enough not to drag in async play.

#### Signaling Weakness Through Fiction

Don't use Chroma resistance charts or vulnerability modifiers. Instead, signal what the opposition responds to through **Scene Words and Cues**. If the Drowned Knight's Scene Words are ⬜ saint, ⬛ chain, 🟦 brine — that tells players which Chroma is relevant without a mechanical chart. Players whose decks and hands align with those colors will have an easier time sourcing Chroma for their Recipes. Players who can't will rely more on Core Words or improvise from their hand. The advantage is natural, not a modifier.

### Beat Structure

A Beat follows this loop:

**1. GM Opens** — Frame the situation. Describe what's changed since last Beat. Provide Scene Words, Cues, and Stitch Words. Set Challenge Ratings for likely actions.

**2. Players Post** — Each player posts once, in any order. They resolve their own rolls and outcomes within their post. Roll consequences are personal: Marks from Misses, Focus spent, Cards discarded.

**3. GM Closes** — Narrate how the players' actions played out together. This is where you spend accumulated Threat, advance or reduce Pressure and Discovery, fill or clear opposition segments, and frame the new situation. Your closing summary *is* the next Beat's opening.

The GM writes **one post per Beat**, not two. The close of one Beat and the open of the next are the same text. This matters — the GM is already the bottleneck in PbP. Don't double their workload.

#### What Happens in the GM's Closing Post

- Narrate the combined fiction of all player posts
- Spend Threat from buckets to introduce complications, enemy actions, or environmental shifts
- Update opposition progress (fill Threat segments, check Trial conditions)
- Advance or hold Pressure and Discovery based on what happened
- Describe what the situation looks like now
- Provide updated Scene Words if the environment changed (including any Breakthrough Scene Words)
- End with a clear Stitch Word

**Roll consequences vs. Threat spends:** These are different things. A player who Misses takes a Mark and generates Threat — that happened in their post, it's done. The GM's Threat spend in the closing post is *additional* — it's the accumulated pressure manifesting as something new. The troll doesn't hit you because you missed. It hits you because enough Peril built up for the GM to make it act. A player who Hit still generated Threat — their success rippled outward and fed the troll's next move.

### Threat Buckets

Track Threat in three buckets. When a Chroma generates Threat, it fills the corresponding bucket:

| Bucket | Fed by | Theme |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Peril** | 🟥 Red, ⬛ Black | Violence escalates, danger closes in |
| **Intrigue** | 🟦 Blue, 🟨 Gold, ⬜ White | The social and informational ground shifts |
| **Dread** | 🟩 Green, 🟪 Violet | The strange and the wrong stirs |

You gain Threat from: Hits (1 Threat, GM assigns bucket), Misses (1 Threat, GM assigns bucket), Breathe posts (Focus recovery option), Risked Wounds, and room triggers.

A player's deck composition tells you which buckets they'll feed. A heavy Red/Black deck fills Peril; a Blue/Gold investigator fills Intrigue. Build your scenes knowing what's coming.

#### Spending Peril

Spend Peril to make danger physical and immediate. The world bites.

- An enemy attacks, repositions, or calls reinforcements
- A route collapses, burns, floods, or locks
- Someone takes a Mark from environmental violence (falling debris, spreading fire, a stampede)
- A weapon, ward, or barrier breaks
- Advance the Pressure track by 1
- Ambush — a hidden threat strikes without warning
- An NPC is harmed, captured, or killed off-screen

**Peril spend scale:** 1 for a complication or single Mark. 2 for a serious escalation or Pressure advance. 3 for a dramatic reversal — someone is Taken Out, the environment transforms, or the situation fundamentally shifts.

#### Spending Intrigue

Spend Intrigue to shift the human landscape. Power, knowledge, and trust rearrange.

- An NPC changes allegiance, withholds aid, or reveals a hidden agenda
- A secret surfaces at the worst possible time
- Authority intervenes — guards arrive, a patron withdraws support, a law is invoked
- Someone's reputation shifts — a rumor spreads, a lie is believed, a truth is dismissed
- Information the party relied on turns out to be wrong or incomplete
- An offer expires or its terms change
- Advance the Pressure track by 1

**Intrigue spend scale:** 1 for a social complication or shifted relationship. 2 for a betrayal, exposed secret, or authority action. 3 for a complete inversion — an ally becomes an enemy, a trusted truth was a lie, or the party's position in the world fundamentally changes.

#### Spending Dread

Spend Dread to make the world wrong. Bodies fail, boundaries blur, the supernatural intrudes.

- The environment decays, warps, or grows where it shouldn't
- A character's body betrays them — illness flares, senses distort, a healed wound reopens
- A ward, seal, or threshold weakens or fails
- Something that was dead, dormant, or sealed shows signs of life
- Visions, omens, or dreams bleed into waking reality
- A supernatural entity notices, approaches, or speaks
- Advance the Pressure track by 1

**Dread spend scale:** 1 for an unsettling sign or minor body horror. 2 for a supernatural intrusion or environmental corruption. 3 for a fundamental violation — reality fractures, a sealed evil wakes, or a character's nature is called into question.

#### General Threat Guidelines

Spend Threat in your closing post at the bottom of each Beat. Threat consequences should change the fiction players respond to next — not override their actions retroactively.

You can bank Threat across Beats. Letting a bucket build creates mounting tension that players can feel even without seeing the number. A sudden 3-point spend from a full bucket is a dramatic turning point.

When the scene doesn't suggest a clear bucket for a Miss or Hit, choose the bucket that's lowest — spread the pressure rather than spiking one track.

---

### Hidden Tracks

Track **Pressure** (rising danger) and **Discovery** (understanding) behind the screen. Players see symptoms, not numbers. When Pressure fills, trigger fallout. When Discovery fills, reveal truth — including hidden Trial conditions.

### Consequences (lightest → heaviest)

Fictional pressure → Mark → Threat → lost Card → Gear Spent/Damaged → Wound → Taken Out

Every Miss produces a Mark and Threat automatically. Additional consequences (lost Cards, Gear damage, Pressure) are at your discretion based on severity. Remember: three Marks convert to a Wound automatically, so even "just a Mark" has cumulative weight. Reserve direct Wounds for filled Pressure triggers, boss actions, and deliberate sacrifice.

Every Hit also generates 1 Threat — success has a cost. The players advance, but the world tightens around them.

### Scene Transitions

**Normal:** Full Focus, draw to hand size, clear Marks, refresh Guard boxes, clear Setup Words.
**Hard** (no breathing room): Focus doesn't reset, draw only 1 Card, Marks carry forward, Guard stays spent. Setup Words persist.

In exploration sites, moving room to room is usually a **Hard Transition**. Call a Normal Transition only when the party has time to breathe, regroup, tend gear, and reorient.

## Act Post Walkthrough

Here's what happens when you write an Act Post, step by step.

> **Situation:** You're in the Flooded Reliquary. Scene Words: 🟨 bell, 🟦 brine, ⬛ drowned, ⬜ saint, 🟥 chain, ⬜ silver. The previous post ended with the word "chain." You're playing Serit Vane and your hand is: 🟥 hunger, ⬜ mercy, 🟦 mirror, 🟨 crown. The GM set CR 9.

1. **Pick your Recipe:** Bell-Cracker Cut (needs 🟥 Red + 🟨 Gold). Core Requirement: violence, weapon, force, command, or sound.
2. **Satisfy the Core Requirement:** Your Core Word *cleave* (tags: violence, weapon, force) provides *violence*. ✓ (You could also satisfy this from a Card or Gear tag — Core Words are your fallback, not your only option.)
3. **Provide Chroma:** Spend 🟥 hunger from your hand (satisfies Red, discard it). Tap Scene Word 🟨 bell (satisfies Gold, not consumed).
4. **Grade:** Tag requirement met + both Chroma components met = **Complete (+2)**.
5. **Stitch?** The previous post ended with "chain" and your post begins with "chain" — you **Stitch** and gain **1 Thread** (0 → 1).
6. **Intensifier?** You declare the **Invocation** Form. Complete → **Heightened (+3)**.
7. **Roll:** 2d6 + 3 vs CR 9. You roll 3 + 3 + 3 = **9**. That meets the CR — **Hit**. You rolled doubles, so you also **recover 1 Focus**.
8. **Resolve:** You get the Hit effect: *Break, silence, interrupt, or disable an object, alarm, voice, weapon, or power source.* The GM gains **1 Threat** from the Hit.
9. **Spend 1 Focus** (3 → 2), then recover 1 from doubles (2 → 3). Your hand is now: ⬜ mercy, 🟦 mirror, 🟨 crown.
10. **Designate a closing word** for the next player's Stitch.

> *Chain rings through my teeth as I bring the blade down. By hunger I name the bell. By silence I claim its voice. By the edge I end its call.* The bronze splits — one clean crack across the face. The note dies halfway to the water.
>
> *The chapel holds its breath. Then: silence.*

Your closing word is **silence**. The Invocation ("By hunger… By silence… By the edge…") qualifies as the Form intensifier. The next player can Stitch from **silence**.

## Setup Post Walkthrough

Here's what happens when you write a Setup Post.

> **Situation:** Same Flooded Reliquary. Your ally Kael is going next and plans to use Quiet Crossing (needs 🟦 Blue + 🟩 Green). You want to help. Your hand is: 🟩 root, ⬛ ash, 🟥 blood, 🟦 frost.

1. **Describe the setup:** You brace open a waterlogged side passage and mark the safe footing with chalk.
2. **Create a Setup Word:** *passage* — colorless by default.
3. **Infuse Chroma?** You spend 🟩 root from your hand to give the Setup Word 🟩 Green Chroma. Now it can satisfy Kael's Green requirement.
4. **Designate a closing word.**

> *The water's only ankle-deep here, but the stones shift underfoot. I wedge my boot against the root and brace the grate open, chalk marks on every stable step. The passage holds.*

Your closing word is **passage**. Kael can Stitch from it *and* tap your 🟩 Setup Word for Quiet Crossing. When Kael uses it, you gain 1 Thread or recover 1 Focus.
